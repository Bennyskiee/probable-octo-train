Summer Holiday

Boracay Beach

Boracay is a small island in the Philippines renowned for its stunning white-sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant nightlife. Located in the Western Visayas region, Boracay is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the Philippines, and is home to a wide range of activities, including swimming, snorkeling, windsurfing, and kitesurfing.

El Nido Palawan

El Nido is a small town and municipality on Palawan Island in the Philippines. It is known for its white-sand beaches, coral reefs and as the gateway to the Bacuit archipelago of islands with steep karst cliffs. Tour boats navigate the limestone caves and lagoons of Miniloc, Dilumacad and Shimizu Islands. Expeditions to nearby snorkeling spots, such as Helicopter Island, Turtle Cave and Secret Lagoon, are popular.

Chocolate Hills

The Chocolate Hills are a unique geological formation in Bohol, Philippines. They are made up of around 1,776 domed hills of a generally conical and almost symmetrical shape, which turn chocolate brown during the dry season, giving them their name.

Undergroud River

The Puerto Princesa Underground River is an 8.2-kilometer-long subterranean river located near the city of Puerto Princesa in Palawan, Philippines. The river winds its way through a spectacular limestone cave before emptying into the South China Sea. Along its route, the river passes through an impressive array of stalactites, stalagmites and other rock formations, creating a breathtaking subterranean landscape.

Banaue Rice Terraces

The Banaue Rice Terraces are an ancient network of terraces carved into the mountains of Ifugao in the Philippines by the ancestors of the indigenous people. The terraces are over 2,000 years old and cover 10,360 square kilometers of mountainside. The terraces are still in use today, providing a sustainable source of food for the local population.

Mayon Volcano

Mayon Volcano is an active stratovolcano located on the Philippine island of Luzon. It is the most active volcano in the Philippines, with 33 recorded eruptions since 1616. It is renowned for its almost perfect cone shape, which is considered to be a symbol of beauty, and is the most photographed and most widely-visited volcano in the country.
